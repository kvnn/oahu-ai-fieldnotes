 # Writing System For O‘ahu A.I. Field Notes

  ## Summary

  Write the book for a technically curious builder-reader: founders, artists, operators, engineers, and serious generalists who want language that
  changes how they see the work. The prose should be field-note witness, not technical documentation: grounded in real artifacts and scenes, but
  shaped into essays with aphoristic titles, durable ideas, and visual rhythm.

  Use A.I. throughout as a deliberate book voice. Keep source grounding mostly invisible in the prose; sources should shape truth, not make the
  chapter feel cited. If time allows, add QR/reference pages later.

  ## Prose Standard

  - Default voice: concrete, observant, exact, and alive. Begin from a real scene, artifact, interface, failure, output, decision, or confusion
    whenever possible.
  - Sentence rule: prefer simple words when they preserve meaning; use precise technical terms when no simpler word is accurate. A reader may need a
    dictionary, but should not need tolerance for jargon.
  - Meaning rule: every paragraph should either reveal a distinction, compress an experience, sharpen a term, complicate an assumption, or move the
    chapter toward a usable idea.
  - Avoid: generic futurism, “AI will transform…” language, tutorial drone, hype cadence, stacked buzzwords, bland productivity claims, and
    overexplained tool inventories.
  - Welcome tension. Good lines should often contain a turn: “Everything in moderation, including moderation” is the title-level standard.

  ## Chapter System

  - Target book shape: 8 chapters, roughly 8 pages each.
  - Visual cadence: at least 2 pages per chapter should give about half the page to an illustration, diagram, image, screenshot, or visual motif.
    Images should be meaning amplifiers first, evidence artifacts second.
  - Use three chapter forms across the book:
      - 4 chapters: scene to principle.
      - 2 chapters: concept first.
      - 2 chapters: notebook mosaic.
  - Default chapter movement:
      - Situation: what happened, what was seen, or what became confusing.
      - Constraint: what made the work hard or strange.
      - Build: what was made, tried, repaired, or discovered.
      - Pattern: the transferable structure underneath.
      - O‘ahu layer: subtle recurring place-awareness, not a forced local section.
      - Field note: the distilled observation.
      - Next build: what this makes possible.

  ## Titles And Concepts

  - Chapter titles should be aphoristic paradoxes: short, memorable, and tension-bearing.
  - Subtitles can carry clarity when the title is compressed.
  - Candidate title standards:
      - “What A.I. Is” can work if the subtitle carries the paradox.
      - “Paradigm Madness” works as a field-note title if the chapter turns chaos into a map.
      - Time-comparison titles like “Conversational A.I. in 2016, 2023, and 2026” should be sharpened into a title plus subtitle.
  - Early chapter pool to develop:
      - What A.I. Is: text in/text out, programmable intelligence, meaning amplifier.
      - Paradigm Madness: filesystem, database, blob, ingress, agents, API, MCP, plugin, webserver, static files, terminals, apps, browsers, buckets,
        LLMs.
      - Conversational A.I. across 2016, 2023, 2026.
      - Programmable video across 2022, 2026.

  ## Codex Drafting Workflow

  - For each chapter, first browse notebooks/files/repos to build an option pool of scenes, artifacts, claims, terms, and visuals.
  - Before drafting prose, create a one-page chapter brief with: title, subtitle, thesis, chapter form, source cluster, page rhythm, visual slots,
    and 5-8 key claims.
  - Draft in Markdown for Vivliostyle, but write for the printed page: paragraphs should have breath, section breaks should feel intentional, and
    visual pages should change the reader’s tempo.
  - Evaluate each draft against: clarity, usefulness, specificity, grounding, opinion strength, local resonance, visual fit, and print rhythm.

  ## Assumptions

  - The reader is a builder-reader.
  - The narrator is a field-note witness.
  - The technical density is layered: readable main prose, precise terms where needed.
  - A.I. is the book style.
  - O‘ahu is a subtle recurring layer, not a mandatory local essay in every chapter.
  - Sources remain mostly invisible unless a detail is narratively useful.
